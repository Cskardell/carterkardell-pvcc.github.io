History

Though Title 54 is the newest title within the U.S code, it is not completely new legislation. The National park service has existed since 1916. In 1970, during efforts to organize and codify many other federal laws, the National Park Service and related programs was originally codified in Title 16. However, in 2014, under president Obama, the code was edited and updated to Title 54.

Organization

Title 54 is generally organized in a Subtitle, Division, Chapter format. The Subtitles are Broad divisions set out to address a large group or system. For instance, Subtitle I addresses the "National Park System", and Subtitle II addresses "Outdoor Recreation Programs". Within those main subtitles is Divisions that outline specific items within each subtitle. For example, Subtitle I has Division A, entitled "Establishment and General Administration" and Division C which deals with "National Heritage Areas". Finally, there are chapters within each division that organize specific qualities of each division. For instance, grants are given out to historic preservation programs, so there is a specific chapter on grants under the "Historic Preservation Division".

Subtitle I Summary: National Park System

Subtitle I: National Park System Subtitle I focuses on the National Park System, which encompasses aspects related to the management, preservation, and administration of national parks. It outlines the establishment and management of national parks, monuments, memorials, and other protected areas, as well as the authorities, responsibilities, and procedures of the National Park Service. This subtitle also addresses matters related to the acquisition of lands for national parks, the protection of park resources, and visitor services within these iconic American landscapes.

Subtitle II Summary: Outdoor Recreation Programs

Subtitle II of Title 54 of the United States Code pertains to Outdoor Recreation Programs. This subtitle covers various initiatives and programs aimed at promoting outdoor recreational activities and experiences for the public. It includes provisions related to federal grants and assistance to state and local governments for outdoor recreational development. The subtitle also addresses the Land and Water Conservation Fund, which supports the acquisition of land for recreational and conservation purposes. Additionally, it outlines the establishment and management of outdoor recreational areas and facilities, as well as the planning and coordination of national outdoor recreation activities.

Subtitle III Summary: National Preservation Programs

Subtitle III of Title 54 of the United States Code focuses on National Preservation Programs. This section primarily deals with the preservation and protection of historic and cultural landmarks and resources. It includes the establishment of the National Register of Historic Places, which recognizes and designates historic properties of significance. The subtitle outlines various programs, incentives, and grants to support the preservation and rehabilitation of historic buildings and areas. It also addresses the responsibilities and authorities of federal agencies in preserving historic resources, as well as the protection of historic properties during federally funded projects. In summary, Subtitle III is dedicated to the conservation and safeguarding of our nation's cultural heritage.
